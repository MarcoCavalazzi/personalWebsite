Spacesuit upgrade project

People who build spacesuits are surely smart, yet they are asking our help. There is a big problem in space we are facing right now: space (ionizing, GCR etc...) radiation. Life on Earth is protected from the full impact of solar and cosmic radiation by the magnetic fields that surround the Earth and by the Earth’s atmosphere, but as we travel farther from Earth’s protective shields we are exposed to the full radiation spectrum and its damaging effects1.
Scientists already figured out some ways to better protect our astronauts from space radiations. The ideas that won NASA's competition were: The winners of the radiation challenge's first round are:
  1. 1st place ($5,000): George Hitt, assistant professor of physics and nuclear engineering at Khalifa University, United Arab Emirates, for proposing a reusable shield that could be placed in an orbit between Earth and Mars.
  2. 2nd Place ($3,000): Ian Gallon, retired researcher in electromagnetics of Bridport, England. He provided mathematical calculations for an active radiation mitigation system.
  3. 3rd Place ($2,000): Olivier Loido, freelance engineer of Toulouse, France. He proposed a launch configuration and a magnet array deployment.
  4. 4th Place ($1,000 each): Markus Novak, recent graduate from Ohio State University of Dublin, Ohio, who found safe areas for travel after performing particle trajectory simulations. Also, Mikhail Petrichenkov of Russia, who described how operations could work using a NASA storm shelter.
In order to conquer space we need a way to shield ourselves and our surroundings from radiations. One of the ideas winnig the NASA radiation challenge suggests that an array of magnets would work. Well, I'm no expert, but electromagnets work everytime a normal magnet works, except that we need to power it up. So we could generate our own electromagnetic field sorrounding the spaceships. A magnetic field strong enough to protect us and controlled enough to not harm us. I'm pretty sure that we can find a way to control it in little time. What we don't have is a way to power up that strong field 24/7. To do so we need to be able to constantly produce an incredible amount of energy. Now, it is true that we could generate energy on Earth and then send it to space using lasers (or something similar), but this approach would impose a huge losses in the transfer. Plus, we don't even have enough energy over here already.
What we need is a generator that works well in space and that produces enough energy to generate that field. If we are successful and the generator is scalable, we could later install a portable version of the generator on the space suits and allow EVA (Extra-Vehicular Activity) without having to worry all the time about radiations and focusing on the job to do.
New constraints for the new energy source are then set:
  • the new generator has to work both on Earth and in space, because we are about to become a multi-planetary specie.
  • it has to be scalable, since we want to use it on the spacesuits
