New Energy

I started this project identifying a big problem I wanted to solve: SMOG.
I came up to the conclusion that the whole thing is the result of the lack of innovation in the energy sector. We know it's dangerous and we have the means to produce electric smog-free vehicles, yet we keep burning oil like there's no tomorrow. So I started to care about this problem... and I came to think about it once a month, once a week, once a day, until I couldn't stop thinking about it. I had to find at least a probable solution.
When talking about "new energy" I mean the kind of energy that can allow all humanity (not only the western countries) to be able to reach self-sustainability while producing 100% clean energy. In more detail, it means that the new energy source must:
  • use no combustion: we don't need to produce pollution, we don't even need to just reduce it, we need to stop it, to put an end to it
  • use no water (like in nuclear power plants): we already lack of drinkable water for everyone, we don't need to waste it
  • use no radioactive material: no fission → no harmful radiations → no problem
  • use no solar panels: I know, solar panels seem great, but how are they produced? And what happens when you have to dispose of them? (read about it)
  • be manufacturable without polluting and be 100% recyclable
Wanna go the extra mile? Check the spacesuit project.
Once the requirements had been set, I started researching how we produce energy today. Then I looked for new proposed ways to produce energy, like harvesting tidal power or creating a pavement that converts all our steps in the house and outside in energy. After reading about all the possibilities and attempts done in the field, I decided which, between the many, was for me the way to go. The one with the highest potential that respected all the constraints. So I made some designs. First in my mind, then as simple drafts and finally as 1:1 scaled 3D models, always improving the final outcome in the matter of chances of success and the amount of material needed to build it (it had to be affordable, not like nuclear fusion). When I reached design number 3 (called "Mark 3") I approached the prototyping phase and failed. That design didn't work... at all. Fortunately, I learned very much from that attempt. I went back in "hardware mode" and remodeled the design, twice. The Mark 5 will see the light soon enough.

Further details will be disclosed as soon as possible.
