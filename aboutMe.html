About me

Let me introduce myself. My name is Marco Carlo Cavalazzi.
I was born in a very small town in the middle of Italy called Casacalenda. At the age of 9 my family moved to Emilia-Romagna (or Po valley). Here I studied to become a Chief Technician in Electronics and Telecommunications.
Even if the diploma had earned me many job offers I felt that in the long run it would not be enough. So there I was, at the University of Bologna, studying Computer Science and Engineering. I did want to study physics; unfortunately that path did not seem to offer the same possibilities for the future.
I managed to get my Bachelor Degree by the end of 2011. At this point I had 3 possible choises: find a job, get the Master's Degree or apply for a Master. Since a company from Milan made the effort to call me and I was willing to put myself in the game I chose to say 'yes' to the job and in the meantime study for the Master's Degree. After a couple of months I found out that the company I was working extra hours for did not care as much for me, so I quit and decided to focus on the studies.
After a year of studies I applied for a position at CERN and I was chosen to be part of the Technical Student Programme. I worked in that awesome international and scientific research environment for 1 year and 2 months. I loved every day there, because it was very challenging and allowed me to grow and meet truly wonderful people, full of passions and initiatives.
After this, I came back to Italy to finish my studies, obtaining my Master's Degree in Computer Science and Engineering with the 110/110 grade with a thesis on Enterprise Social Networks: the case of CERN.
